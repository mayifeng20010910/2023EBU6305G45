Olympic Park

From： baike.baidu.com

Located at the northernmost point of Axis of Beijing extension, Olympic Forest Park is the largest urban green landscape in Asia, covering an area of about 680 hectares, making it a park larger than the Yuanmingyuan (Old Summer Place) and the Summer Palace combined. Its construction began in 2003 and was officially completed on July 3, 2008.

The North Fifth Ring Road crosses the middle of the park, dividing it into the Northern Garden and the Southern Garden, connected by an ecological bridge spanning the Fifth Ring Road and full of plants. The Southern Garden is dominated by a large natural landscape, while the Northern Garden is dominated by a small stream landscape and a natural wild forest, making it a worthy "green lung" of Beijing's urban area.

The most famous landscapes in the forest park are "The Yangshan Hill" and "Lake Aohai". "Yangshan" is the main peak of the park, echoing the "Jingshan Mountain" on the central axis of Beijing. The main lake of the park is called "Aohai", which is a reference to the traditional name of Beijing's lakes, which are mostly named after "sea(hai)". And the word "Ao(奥)" is a reference to the word "Olympic（奥林匹克）", with the meaning of the mystery and secret, and it also has a wonderful meaning of the sea of Olympics. "Yangshan" and "Aohai", meaning "high mountain and long river", imply that the Olympic spirit endures and the Chinese cultural traditions flourish.
