Ming City Wall Ruins Park

The Ming City Wall Ruins Park is located at No. 9 Chongwenmen East Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing, covering an area of approximately 86 hectares with a water surface of 8.6 hectares. The total area of the Ming City Wall Ruins Park is approximately 15.5 hectares. The Beijing Ming City Wall Ruins Park covers an area of 3.3 hectares with the city wall ruins and the southeast corner tower, and the green space area of the Beijing Ming City Wall Ruins Park is 12.2 hectares. In terms of garden construction, the main design concept is to create a changing and ancient environment. A total of over 70000 square meters of lawn have been planted, 110000 flowers have been planted, over 400 large-sized trees such as Chinese pine and Chinese locust have been planted, and over 6000 various shrubs have been planted. We have laid various pipelines with an extension of 28000 meters and completed the installation of night lighting equipment for city walls, green spaces, and nearly 10000 square meters of footpaths, sidewalks, corridors, and squares.

Address:

9 Chongwenmen East Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing

Highlights:

The ruins of the city wall and the southeast corner of the city, with garden scenery

the Palace Museum

The Forbidden City is located at No. 4 Jingshanqian Street, Dongcheng District, also known as the "Forbidden City". It was the imperial palace of the Ming and Qing dynasties and is also the largest and most complete existing royal ancient architectural complex in China. The Forbidden City has four gates, with the Meridian Gate to the south, the Shenwu Gate to the north, the Donghua Gate to the east, and the Xihua Gate to the west. At each corner of the city wall, there are graceful turrets, and there is a folk saying of nine beams, eighteen columns, and seventy-two ridges, describing their complex structure. In the majestic royal palace, learn about China's glorious history and rich culture, and search for the glory and luxury of the past in the story and legend.

Address:

4 Jingshanqian Street, Dongcheng District, Beijing

Highlights:

Palace buildings such as the Meridian Gate, Cining Palace, and Imperial Garden
