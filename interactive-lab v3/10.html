Beijing Zoo

From： baike.baidu.com

Beijing Zoo was built in 1906, and is located in the "Agricultural Experiment Field" outside Xizhimen, which was originally the "two gardens and two temples" of Le Shan Garden, Ji Garden, Guang Shan Temple and Hui An Temple. It was prepared by the Ministry of Commerce by decree and consists of three parts: the zoo, the botanical garden, and the agricultural experiment. The earliest zoo is located on the east side of the park, covering an area of 1.5 hectares, wich was the earliest zoo open to the public in China and the earliest park open to the public in North China. It is also said to be the birthplace of modern zoos, botanical gardens and museums in China.
