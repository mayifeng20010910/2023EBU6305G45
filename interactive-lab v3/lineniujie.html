Niujie Mosquek

The Niujie Mosque is located on Niujie Street within Guang'an Gate. It is the largest and oldest mosque in Beijing, with main buildings including the worship hall, Bangge Tower, Moon Watching Tower, and Stele Pavilion. The main existing buildings in the temple were all built during the Ming and Qing dynasties, which is a typical example of a mosque built using traditional Han architectural forms.

Pan Jiayuan

The Panjiayuan second-hand market is open every Monday to Friday for shop merchants, as well as for the second and western stalls in the first and second areas of the greenhouse. All shop stalls are open on Saturdays and Sundays, and the market is open all year round, with extremely high popularity. People from 24 provinces and cities across the country have set up stalls here to operate, and many ethnic minorities also operate their own ethnic products here. Due to its superior geographical location, fast and convenient transportation, strong folk characteristics, and flexible trading methods, the market is deeply loved by both Chinese and foreign guests. The market is known by news media as the "most popular antique market in China", "the most comprehensive collection market in China", and "the largest distribution center for folk handicrafts in China".
