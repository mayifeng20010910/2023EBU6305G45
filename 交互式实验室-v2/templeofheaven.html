The Temple of Heaven

From： baike.baidu.com

Temple of Heaven Park, formerly known as "Temple of Heaven", is located at No.7 Dongli inside the Temple of Heaven, Dongcheng District, Beijing. It was built in the 18th year of Yongle of Ming Dynasty (1420) and renamed "Temple of Heaven" in the 9th year of Jiajing of Ming Dynasty (1530). It is a place for emperors of Ming and Qing dynasties to "worship heaven" and pray for grain ", with a total area of 273 hectares.It is the largest extant ancient sacrificial building complex in China. On January 1 in the seventh year of the Republic of China (1918), the Temple of Heaven Park was opened to the public.

The Temple of Heaven Park is surrounded by two altar walls, divided into "inner altar" and "outer altar" two parts; The north is round, the south is square, meaning "heaven round place"; It mainly consists of four ancient buildings, namely, the "Prayer Altar for good Harvest" in spring, the "Circular Mound Altar" for worshipping heaven on winter solstice, the fasting palace where the emperor lived before worship, and the Kagura Office where ritual music was practiced. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest in the Altar of Prayer for Grain is a landmark building in Beijing. The Temple of Heaven Park has a lot of ancient Berlin planted outside the altar.

On May 8, 2007, the Temple of Heaven Park was rated as a national AAAAA tourist attraction by the National Tourism Administration.
